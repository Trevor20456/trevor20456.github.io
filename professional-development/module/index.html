Self-guided learning pathway

Four Steps to an AI Literacy Activity

Open each section in order. Keep one upcoming lesson and one decision students should be able to explain in mind as you work.

01
QuestionBegin with human judgment

Choose a lesson in which students already evaluate information, evidence or consequences. Finish this sentence: “Students should not accept an AI response until they have…”

Your checkpoint

One content objective and one reason AI literacy matters within that objective.

02
VerifyCompare output with credible evidence

Provide an AI-generated statement, summary or image and at least two credible sources students can use to check it. Ask students to identify what is accurate, missing, uncertain or misleading.

  • What evidence confirms the claim?
  • What information is missing?
  • What source would you trust most, and why?
03
EvaluateExamine bias, privacy and consequences

Ask students to consider who benefits, who could be harmed, what data are involved and what assumptions shape the output. Avoid requiring students to enter personal information into an AI system.

Access check

Offer the AI output inside the lesson so participation does not depend on every student creating an account or accepting a platform’s terms.

04
ApplyMake and defend a responsible decision

Students revise the AI output, decide whether it should be used or write a short policy recommendation. Their final response should cite evidence and make the human reasoning visible.

Final product

A Classroom-Ready AI Literacy Activity

  • Aligned to one content objective
  • Uses credible comparison sources
  • Addresses bias or privacy
  • Requires a reasoned student decision

Concrete examples

Three Social Science Pathways

These examples come from my AI Literacy in Action artifact and can be adapted to other content areas.

U.S. History

Verify a historical summary

Compare an AI summary with class notes and a primary source, then identify what it gets right, leaves out or misrepresents.

Government

Evaluate an ethical scenario

Examine AI use in schools, policing, hiring or campaigns and recommend safeguards for fairness, privacy and accountability.

World History

Use AI as a starting point

Research a historical question, verify the response and explain when the tool was useful and when it was limited.
