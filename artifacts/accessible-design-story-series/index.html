EDEL 518A Design Project · Standard 4.6

Accessible Design Story Series

A three-part visual resource showing how accessibility can be planned throughout an instructional-design process rather than added after a digital lesson is finished.

Embedded artifact

CSUF-Inspired Story Series

The three original story pages are presented below as responsive, selectable text so the artifact remains readable with screen readers, keyboard navigation and text enlargement.

Story 1

Design for Access From the Start

Use readable text, captions, descriptive structure, flexible participation, keyboard access and layouts that work on mobile devices.

Story 2

Accessibility Throughout ADDIE

Identify learner needs during analysis, plan multiple pathways during design, test accessibility during development, support access during implementation and use feedback during evaluation.

Story 3

A Practical Design Check

Confirm true text, sufficient contrast, meaningful alternative text, captions, multiple ways to participate and successful testing on real devices.

Accessibility choice: Rebuilding the visual story series as semantic HTML preserves the content while avoiding barriers created by image-only text.

Sharing and evidence plan

How I Will Share This With Colleagues

Start with a useful self-check

I will share the story series during a short staff conversation or through a digital resource page, then invite colleagues to use its five checks on one lesson they already teach. This keeps the conversation practical and avoids turning accessibility into another abstract compliance task.

Build honest evidence over time

I will collect voluntary feedback about which checks uncovered a barrier, what educators revised and how students responded. I will not collect student-identifying information. That evidence can later show how data were gathered securely, interpreted for design decisions and used to help learners identify supports that work for them.
