Standard 4.1.d Artifact · Upcoming role

Trona High School Technology Recognition Plan

A future-facing system for recognizing educators who use technology to strengthen learning in grades 7–12.

Listening first

A Plan for My Upcoming Position

I will begin at Trona High School as a new colleague and emerging technology coach. I do not yet have enough first-hand knowledge to name educators or describe their classroom practice accurately, so this artifact establishes the process I will follow after onboarding.

01

Observe and Listen

First 60 days

I will learn from school leaders, the district’s technology support, classroom observations and informal conversations. The goal is to understand what is already working instead of arriving with assumptions.

02

Invite and Confirm

Voluntary recognition

I will invite examples or nominations, record the learning need and evidence of impact, and ask each educator to review the wording before a spotlight is published.

03

Celebrate and Share

Community learning

Each short spotlight will name a practice, explain why it matters and offer one idea another educator could adapt. Recognition becomes a resource for professional learning, not simply an award.

04

Look for High-Impact Practice

Learning · access · leadership

I will look for technology that deepens inquiry, feedback, collaboration or student agency; reduces barriers; and helps educators share useful practice with colleagues.

Artifact status

Ready for Onboarding

This planning artifact will be updated with real, educator-approved examples after I begin at Trona High School. Keeping the language in future tense makes the evidence honest while still showing a clear approach to Standard 4.1.d.
