EDEL 523 Artifact · Standards 4.4 and 4.7

Distance Education & AUP Quick Brief

This presentation translates a real district acceptable-use policy into practical guidance for distance education. It is used twice in this portfolio because the same work demonstrates different aspects of learning design and digital citizenship.

AUP

Original presentation

A Policy Made Usable

The brief focuses on Covina-Valley Unified School District’s Student Internet and Technology Acceptable Use guidance, revised in July 2025. It helps educators connect policy language to the everyday decisions students make in a distance-education environment.

  • Responsible participation and communication online
  • Privacy, personal data and digital identity
  • Clear expectations for safe use of school technology
  • Practical classroom implementation rather than policy in isolation

How the Evidence Is Used

Standard 4.4.a and 4.4.c

Learning Designer

The brief grounds distance-education design in an authentic policy and gives educators a concise resource they can use when planning active online learning. Its segmented visual format also offers a more accessible entry point than asking learners to navigate a full policy document without guidance.

Standard 4.7.d

Digital Citizen Advocate

The presentation makes privacy and acceptable use part of instruction. It supports informed decisions about personal data and digital profiles instead of assuming students will understand those responsibilities simply because they use technology often.
