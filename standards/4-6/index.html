ISTE Coach Standard 4.6

Data-Driven Decision-Maker

Coaches model and support the use of qualitative and quantitative data to inform their own instruction and professional learning.

Review the complete official standard ↗

Standard overview

Indicators

Plain-language summaries are provided below. The official ISTE page remains the source of record.

4.6.a

Secure data practices

Model responsible ways to collect, protect and analyze student data.

4.6.b

Interpret evidence

Help educators use multiple forms of data to make decisions and support individual learners.

4.6.c

Student ownership of data

Help educators empower students to use learning evidence to set goals and monitor progress.

Direct alignment · 4.6.a–c

How the Project Addresses Every Indicator

4.6.a

Use Data Responsibly

The guide tells students and educators to look for patterns rather than labels, consider context such as language, access, health and responsibilities, ask what the numbers do not show, and use data to support growth. Student worksheets should be stored privately and discussed without publicizing personally identifiable information.

4.6.b

Combine Evidence for Decisions

Students pair a measurable result—such as a score, percentage, level or completed assignments—with a reflection about their learning. Notice–Ask–Act uses both forms of evidence to identify gaps, consider missing information, and choose an appropriate next step.

4.6.c

Build Student Ownership

Students use their own evidence to write a SMART goal, choose a strategy and support, define how success will be measured, and complete a check-in reflection. Monitoring progress and adjusting the plan become part of the learner’s work.
