ISTE Coach Standard 4.6

Data-Driven Decision-Maker

Coaches model and support responsible use of qualitative and quantitative evidence to guide instruction and professional learning.

Review the complete official standard ↗

Standard overview

Indicators

Plain-language summaries are provided below. The official ISTE page remains the source of record.

4.6.a

Secure data practices

Model responsible ways to collect, protect and analyze student data.

4.6.b

Interpret evidence

Help educators use multiple forms of data to make decisions and support individual learners.

4.6.c

Student ownership of data

Help educators empower students to use learning evidence to set goals and monitor progress.

My EDEL 518A design project provides a practical resource for examining access throughout the design process. The sharing plan below explains how this artifact can lead to responsible evidence collection and revision.

EDEL 518A design project

Accessible Design Story Series

Three visual story pages connect accessibility to the ADDIE process and offer five checks educators can use before publishing a digital lesson.

Sharing plan

From project to colleague self-check

I will share the series with colleagues and invite them to apply its five checks to one existing digital lesson. Short, voluntary feedback will identify which barriers were found and what was revised, without collecting student-identifying information.

Planned alignment · evidence will be added after sharing

Connections to Indicators 4.6.a–c

4.6.a

Collect securely

Use an anonymous accessibility self-check and avoid student names or personally identifying information.

4.6.b

Interpret evidence

Look for patterns in the barriers educators identify and use those patterns to guide future support and design revisions.

4.6.c

Support learner ownership

Adapt the five checks into learner-friendly questions students can use to identify access needs and monitor whether a resource works for them.
